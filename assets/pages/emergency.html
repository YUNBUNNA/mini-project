Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our services

Emergency responders can usually access your information within seconds to a couple of minutes, depending on how and where it’s stored. If it’s on a visible medical ID, bracelet, or in your phone’s emergency section, they can see it almost immediately. If it’s in a medical database, it may take a few minutes to retrieve.

Your medical information is HIPAA compliant and secure only if it’s stored and managed by a healthcare provider, hospital, or service that follows HIPAA regulations. Information you keep on a phone, bracelet, or personal app is not covered by HIPAA, so while it may still be safe, it’s not legally protected in the same way.

If you’re unconscious and can’t provide information, emergency responders will look for it in visible or accessible places—such as a medical ID bracelet, wallet card, phone emergency info, or through hospital/EMS databases—so they can quickly learn your identity, medical conditions, allergies, and emergency contacts.
